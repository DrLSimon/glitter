    • Part 1: Parametric surface

    In this part you will need to implement the two methods named PA3Application::makeASphere and PA3Application::makeATorus. To help you in this tasks, here is a coarse algorithmic skeleton for discretizing a surface parametrized as $P(\phi,\theta)$. 

    $points \leftarrow \emptyset$
$faces \leftarrow \emptyset$
for $k_\phi\in\{0,\cdots,n-1\}$ do
  for $k_\theta\in\{0,\cdots,n-1\}$ do
    $\phi=\phi_0+k_\phi\delta_\phi$
    $\theta=\theta_0+k_\theta\delta_\theta$
    $points \leftarrow  points \cup \{ P(\phi,\theta)\}$
    if ($k_\phi\leq n-2$ or isCyclicInPhi) and ($k_\phi\leq n-2$ or isCyclicInTheta) then
      # $1^\text{st}$ triangle
      $faces \leftarrow faces \cup \{\mathrm{index}(k_\phi, k_\theta), \mathrm{index}(k_\phi+1,k_\theta), \mathrm{index}(k_\phi,k_\theta+1)\}$   
      # $2^\text{nd}$ triangle
      $faces \leftarrow faces \cup \{\mathrm{index}(k_\phi,k_\theta+1), \mathrm{index}(k_\phi+1,k_\theta), \mathrm{index}(k_\phi+1,k_\theta+1)\}$
    fi
  done
done
    In this algorithm, $\mathrm{index}(k_\phi, k_\theta)$ is the index location of $P(\phi,\theta)$ in the list of points. Beware its definition at the "boundaries" : for instance in the sphere example, the index behaviour is cyclic with respect to $\phi$ but not with respect to $\theta$, that is to say: $P(2\pi, \theta)=P(0,\theta)$ but $P(\phi,\frac \pi 2) \neq P(\phi,-\frac \pi 2)$. This cyclic / non cyclic behaviour is denoted by the boolean expressions: isCyclicInPhi and isCyclicInTheta.
